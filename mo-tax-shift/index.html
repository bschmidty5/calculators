HJR 173 Rate-Cut Trigger Table
Under HJR 173: each $20M above FY2025 base of $13.43B GR cuts the top income tax rate by 0.01 pp (max 1.6 pp/yr). Rate goes to zero when it falls below 1.4%. Legislature has 3 years post-ballot approval to expand the tax base. The new unified sales tax must replace both the $9.17B income tax and the $3.22B state sales tax — combined target $12.39B.

Sources & Methodology: FY2025 actuals from MO Office of Administration: income tax $9.17B, state sales/use tax $3.22B; combined target $12.39B. Missouri total PCE ~$295B (BEA 2023 scaled). Housing: Mortgage interest and principal are exempt under virtually all sales tax structures. Business Inputs — Currently Taxable: These are already in the $3.22B base (§144.030 does not exempt non-manufacturing business purchases — office supplies, restaurant gear, telecom, general facility utilities, construction materials for non-exempt entities). Business Inputs — Currently Exempt: Manufacturing machinery, raw materials, ag inputs, and goods-for-resale are exempt under §144.030 & §144.054. Taxing goods-for-resale ($18.5B) would cause severe tax pyramiding. B2B Services — Currently Exempt: Missouri exempts most services from sales tax, with narrow exceptions (telecom, fabrication labor). Professional & business services is Missouri's largest GDP sector at $52.1B; buyer-side B2B service spending across MO businesses is estimated at ~$55–65B. Taxing B2B services risks tax cascading — costs embed in final consumer prices — and most states that broadly tax services carve out B2B transactions. HJR 173 gives the legislature broad authority with no guardrails; legislators have named personal services (haircuts, plumbers) as likely initial targets, not B2B services. Business telecom is already taxable and defaulted on. All other B2B services default to exempt. HJR 173 passed MO House 98–54 March 12, 2026; Senate pending. For policy analysis only.
